Servings: 2 people for 3 days   |   Cook Time: ~1 hour

üßÇ Ingredients

Steak

  • 6 New York strip steaks
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 4 cloves of garlic (for two steaks)
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Fresh thyme
  • Butter

Baked Potato

  • 6 large potatoes
  • Bacon (turkey, pork, or beef based on preference)
  • Sour cream
  • Green onion
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Mozzarella cheese

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

1. Wash your potatoes with cold water, rinse well, and pat dry. Place them on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil.
2. Preheat the oven to 400¬∞F. Puncture each potato six times on each side with a fork.
3. Once the oven is ready, place the potatoes inside and bake for 25 minutes.
4. Melt 4 tablespoons of butter. Brush the potatoes and season heavily with salt. Return to oven for another 30 minutes.
5. Cut bacon into small pieces and cook in a pan until crispy.
6. With 15 minutes left on the timer, start cooking the steak.
7. Clean and pat dry the steaks. Season generously with salt and pepper.
8. Warm up a pan with a bit of oil on medium-high heat. Sear each side until golden brown.
9. Lower the heat to medium-low and add 5 tablespoons of butter, garlic cloves, fresh thyme, and fresh rosemary. Cook for another 6 minutes for medium doneness.
10. Once potatoes are done, slice them in the middle. Add butter, salt, pepper, and mozzarella cheese, mixing the inside.
11. Plate your potatoes with your steak, topping with sour cream, bacon, and green onions. Enjoy!
